Frequently Asked Questions

Why are you transitioning from academia into data science?

I chose to undertake graduate work because academic science was one of the paths to positive social impact which I had a strong aptitude for — given my analytical and critical thinking, ability to quickly synthesise new material, and curiosity and thirst to continuously learn new things.

However, I learned that much of academic science is hindered by a general lack of funding and positions for advancement, less ambitious research goals stemming from the publish-or-perish attitude, and dependence on processes of the established institutions.

So shortly after graduating from the University of Cambridge, I decided to use the skills I’d developed — in combination with newly acquired data science skills — to contribute to the society in the infinitely more free, growing and dynamic environment outside of academia.

What excites you about data science?

I’m excited about the richness of opportunities we have to explore diverse data sets with freely available open source tools to achieve positive social impact in many areas of our lives!

My colleague uses neural networks to predict the toxicity of cancer treatment to increase patient survival.

Data Science for Social Good fellows work out models to predict violations of safely handling hazardous waste.

Nathan Kallus uses social media data to predict violent crowd behaviour.

These are some examples of the exciting work I’m working toward being a part of.
